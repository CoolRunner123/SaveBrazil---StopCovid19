OVERVIEW

Problem: Amidst the COVID19 pandemic, illegal loggers are deforesting the State of Amazonas and spreading the disease to indigenous groups. This deforestation also increases the risk of indigenous groups dying from the disease, as the poorer air quality weakens the respiratory system. By the rapid spread of COVID-19 throughout the State of Amazonas, these low socioeconomic regions are vastly affected, and we need to take action.

Why is this important? Due to the increasingly high rates of deforestation as well as the poor socioeconomic condition in the Amazon, the percent of population of indigenous groups affected by COVID19 is increasing rapidly.

Rate of COVID-19 Cases vs. Days after 26 February 2020





As depicted by the future model, the rate of the cases in the Amazon region is proven to rise to a higher rate than the other non-Amazonian regions . It is bizarre to see these results because the population density is so low in the Amazon, and COVID-19 is proven to spread through droplet transmission according to the CDC. Upon further analysis, it becomes clear that there are additional factors involved such as the higher particulate matter concentration and the lower socio-economic groups in the area that have a positive correlation with these findings.









To further research the variables affecting particulate matter in the vast region of the Amazon, we noticed that the recent rise of deforestation in the region had a positive correlation with the particulate matter concentration (rsp) . We used Earth Observatory Data from NASA to track the specific deforestation loss in the region, and JAXA/EORC Real-Time Monitoring to visualize the rsp ranges in these regions. Although the deforestation here goes up to 2019, 2020 was reported to have 7,700km^2 (99% was illegal) of deforestation in the Amazon in 2020 according to Brazil's Space Research Institute . After understanding that the regions near the deforestation have higher rsp values AND higher cases of COVIDd-19 as deforestation continues to proceed, it strengthens the argument that increased rsp levels contribute to worsening immune responses to fight COVID-19 as stated by the CDC . This is putting the environment and the people in this region at extreme risk .
